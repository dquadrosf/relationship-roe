Methodology a credit officer can cite. Relationship ROE is after-tax profit measured against the equity capital the bank must hold for the relationship's risk. Net interest income is the spread between each asset's yield and its matched funding cost (FTP), plus the spread between the FTP credit on the client's deposits and the rate paid to them. We add fee income (upfront fees amortized over the facility life; recurring fees taken annually), subtract allocated operating expense and expected loss (PD × LGD × EAD, with credit-conversion factors on unfunded commitments and letters of credit), and apply the tax rate. Capital is allocated as risk-weighted assets × target capital ratio. ROE = after-tax net income ÷ allocated capital; RAROC expresses the pre-tax risk-adjusted return over economic capital; both are compared to the cost-of-equity hurdle. NPV discounts the relationship's after-tax cash flows over its life, recognizing upfront fees at year 0.

Not official numbers. Defaults (tax rate, capital ratio, hurdle, risk weights, the optional PD/LGD grid) are illustrative placeholders. Replace them with the bank's own policy figures before this goes into a real credit decision.
